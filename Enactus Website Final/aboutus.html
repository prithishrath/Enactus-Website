Our History

Formed in february 2011 , enactus dtu is a dedicated team of students with a head for bussiness and a heart for the world . The team truly believes in empowerment of the weaker sections of the society through busines acumen and diligence . it is this belief which has allowed enactus dtu to run over 10 flourishing projects across the CR. Enactus dtu has constantly endeavoured to be the mascot of change and revolution, and bring innovative entrepreneurial solutions to the table in matters concerning the various evils afflicting the indian society . This approach has brought Enactus multiple laurels of success.Enactus DTU represented india in the world cup 2013 and recetly placed 3rd in india, int the enactus national competition 20177. We secured the fifth position in the enactus national competition 2018 out of 24 competing teams.

Enactus DTU

Enactus DTU, ever since its inception,has been an institution committed not only to change, but to sustainable change. Enactus DTU consists of a carefully curated and dedicated team of students with a head for business and a heart for the world, who are enthusiastic to work for the upliftment of the society.One of our first targeted ventures, Project Chaap, not only granted several slum women of Pitampura employment as custom T-shirt makers, but also impressed the world and led Enactus DTU to victory at Enactus Nationals 2016, along with Project Ann and Project Unmoolan. From teaching acid attack victims to make soap from waste cooking oil through Project Avtaran, installing filters and facilitating the supply of clean drinking water to slum dwellers through Project Unnayan, promoting literacy through digital means under the flagship Project Unnati, Enactus DTU has well leveraged our teams engineering and scientific research abilities. Our team is novel in the way that it is made up of both engineering and management students, lending the team a dual technical-commercial perspective to view problems in. Our most recent endeavors include the groundbreaking Project Rangmanch, an attempt to revive the dying art of puppetry, and a one-of-itâ€™s-kind collaborative venture Project Milan, devised in association with Enactus Southampton, which aims to promote aquaponics as a profitable means of agriculture in a water scarce country like ours. Ever since the first idea and the first plan, our team has never looked back - Enactus DTU has become a brand commonly associated with innovation, impact and long lasting social change.

Our Vision

The biggest difference between an NGO and a social entrepreneurship organisation is one thing,Self sufficiency. Pertaining to social welfare, self sufficiency is crucial because it ensures sustainable social impact. The business models we create have 3 equally important parts. The customer. The product/service. The entrepreneur.Our vision focuses on looking for a product or a service that is essential to good living standards. We then focus on bringing this necessity to the financially and socially unfortunate sections of the society. We do this by interviewing several candidates who show potential and zeal that they want to do something better with their lives. People who could never rise above their circumstances because they never had the right tools, guidance and right people at their disposal. We give them a chance at an honest means of earning their livelihood as well as a shot at improving people's lives, all at the same time. We train them in the art of making their product attractive, driving in people through publicity and how to ensure a steady income. After our entrepreneurs learn how to run their establishment on their own, we replicate the process till we have reached as many people as possible through our endeavours. Enactus DTU strives to constantly look for new opportunities of promoting social welfare through entrepreneurial spirit.

Our Achievements

Every year Enactus DTU grows from what it currently is and strives higher. Through our various projects, we target the underprivileged sections of the society and bring about a change. Through our project Rangmanch, there are 4000 people directely impacted by work and 80,000 more people indirectly impacted. The project has spread 5 social messages and also helps entrepreneurs as reported 12 entreprenuers enjoy social security benefit and also 2 self help groups were created. The economical impact of is 12 entreprenuers having an income of INR 13,000 per entrepreneur. There were 18 shows conducted. Thus for the entrepreneurs almost 108% increase in income ( INR 6,000-INR13,000) was seen. Project Unnati has manged to set up 10 cafes over different locations in Delhi while having 11 enterpreneurs working with it and offering 32 courses. The income generated per entreprenuer is INR 14,800 per Month. Apart from that, Project Unnati has managed to save 5,50,000 pages while reducing 21 tonnes carbon footprint and powering 1,00,000 rural households. The amount is students placed by this project is 250 students and developing an Unnati Community of about 2,75,000 people. We don't stop here but continue to impact society with our already sustainable projects.
